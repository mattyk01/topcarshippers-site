What Is RoadRunner Auto Transport?

RoadRunner Auto Transport is an auto transport broker and carrier that has been shipping vehicles since 1996. They operate a proprietary load board — a private marketplace connecting shippers with vetted carriers — and an in-house team of transport coordinators, dispatch specialists, and shipment support agents. They are licensed with the FMCSA under USDOT number 3124544.

Unlike most brokers, who post your vehicle to a shared marketplace and wait for a carrier to respond, RoadRunner actively manages the matching process. Their in-house team stays with your shipment from booking through delivery, which means someone who knows your order is reachable throughout the process.

Pricing and Quotes

RoadRunner offers instant online quotes through their car shipping calculator. You can get a price in under 60 seconds without providing a phone number first, which is less common in this industry than it should be.

Pricing is transparent upfront. The quote you receive at booking reflects what you will pay; there are no deposit requirements and no bait-and-switch adjustments at pickup. You pay only when a carrier is confirmed for your shipment. For most standard routes, RoadRunner's pricing is competitive with the broader market — not always the absolute lowest, but fair relative to the service level.

Factors that affect your final price include route distance, vehicle type and size, transport method (open vs. enclosed), time of year, and pickup flexibility. Peak season (roughly May through September) tends to run higher; booking two to three weeks out typically yields better pricing than last-minute requests.

Carrier Network and Vetting

RoadRunner's carrier network is one of their strongest differentiators. They maintain a proprietary load board with over 14,000 vetted carriers, which they describe as screened for safety record, insurance compliance, and delivery performance. Carriers are required to meet FMCSA licensing requirements and pass RoadRunner's internal vetting process before being dispatched on a shipment.

This matters because the carrier — not the broker — is the entity physically handling your vehicle. A broker with a poor carrier network produces poor outcomes regardless of how good their customer service is. RoadRunner's active dispatch model means they are selecting carriers from their vetted pool rather than broadcasting to whoever responds first on a shared marketplace.

Coverage and Transport Options

RoadRunner ships to all 50 states, including Alaska and Hawaii, as well as Puerto Rico and the Caribbean. This is broader coverage than most brokers offer without routing through a specialty partner.

Transport options include:

  • Open carrier — standard transport on a multi-car trailer; suitable for most vehicles
  • Enclosed transport — covered trailer for luxury, exotic, classic, or condition-sensitive vehicles
  • Door-to-door delivery — available in most markets; terminal service available where door-to-door is not practical

Real-time tracking is available through their online portal, allowing you to monitor shipment status throughout the move.

Customer Experience

RoadRunner's in-house team model produces a more consistent customer experience than pure brokers who hand off to third parties entirely. Customers have direct contact with coordinators who know their shipment details, which matters when questions or issues arise during transit.

On-time performance is solid on standard routes. Pickup windows can extend during peak season when carrier demand is high — this is an industry-wide pattern, not specific to RoadRunner, but worth noting for time-sensitive moves. Door-to-door delivery is not available in all rural markets; in those cases, terminal pickup and delivery is the alternative.
