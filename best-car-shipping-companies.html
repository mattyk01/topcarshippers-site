Best Car Shipping Companies of 2026

We evaluated dozens of auto transport providers on pricing transparency, carrier vetting, customer communication, and actual delivery performance. These are the ten that earned a spot.

10 companies ranked · Updated April 2026 · No paid placements

Shipping a car sounds straightforward until you're actually doing it. Quotes vary by hundreds of dollars for the same route, pickup windows slip, and some companies quote one price and collect another. After evaluating dozens of auto transport providers, these are the ten companies that earned a spot on this list.

RoadRunner Auto Transport is our top pick. They have been in this business for 30 years, they do not play games with pricing, and their in-house team actively manages your shipment rather than handing it off and disappearing. The nine companies below them are worth considering depending on your situation: budget, vehicle type, timeline, and how much support you want along the way.

1. RoadRunner Auto Transport Best Overall
★★★★★ 4.9 / 5
Top-rated car shipping company for 2026

RoadRunner has been shipping cars for 30 years and that operational history shows. They run a proprietary load board and an in-house team of transport coordinators, dispatch specialists, and shipment support agents who stay with your order from booking through delivery. Pricing is transparent upfront with no deposit required at booking; you pay only when a carrier is confirmed. They offer both open and enclosed transport, door-to-door delivery in most markets, and real-time tracking. For the majority of standard shipments, RoadRunner offers the best combination of price, reliability, and customer accountability.

2. Easy Auto Ship
★★★★★ 4.7 / 5
Smooth, low-friction booking with thoughtful extras

Easy Auto Ship has built a reputation around making the process genuinely simple. Quotes are easy to get, the booking flow is clean, and their customer service team is consistently responsive. They include a few extras that many competitors charge for or skip entirely: rental car delay coverage and a free car wash on qualifying shipments. A great fit for first-time shippers who want a frictionless experience from quote to delivery.

Pros

  • Low-friction, well-designed booking process
  • Rental delay coverage on qualifying shipments
  • Consistently responsive customer service
  • Strong fit for first-time shippers

Cons

  • Mid-range pricing; not always the cheapest option
  • Coverage extras have eligibility conditions
3. SGT Auto Transport
★★★★★ 4.7 / 5
Reliable value across both open and enclosed routes

SGT has built a broad carrier network and consistently competitive pricing on standard routes. Booking is straightforward, and one standout feature is the ability to select a specific pickup date rather than the loose pickup window most competitors offer, which is a meaningful convenience for shippers working around a tight schedule. Customer reviews are solid across the board.

Pros

  • Competitive pricing across most routes
  • Specific pickup date selection available
  • Broad, reliable carrier network
  • Straightforward booking process

Cons

  • Less personalized than full-service options
  • Enclosed transport less of a specialty
4. Nexus Auto Transport
★★★★★ 4.6 / 5
Strong marks for pickup and delivery reliability

Nexus has earned some of the best reviews in the industry specifically for the reliability and efficiency of their pickups and deliveries, an area where many competitors fall short. Pricing is competitive on standard routes, coverage is nationwide, and customer service is consistently reachable. A dependable choice for shippers who prioritize a smooth, on-time experience over chasing the lowest possible price.

Pros

  • Strong reputation for on-time pickup and delivery
  • Competitive pricing on standard routes
  • Nationwide coverage; open and enclosed
  • Reachable, responsive customer service

Cons

  • No major specialty differentiator beyond reliability
  • Variable experience depending on assigned carrier
5. AmeriFreight
★★★★☆ 4.4 / 5
Established broker with generous discount programs

AmeriFreight is one of the more established names in the industry, with a long operational history and a discount program that stands out: military, students, first responders, and repeat customers can all qualify for meaningful savings. Their pricing model includes an optional insurance gap coverage add-on. A strong choice if you qualify for a discount or value a company with a long track record.

Pros

  • Strong discount programs (military, students, first responders)
  • Long operational history
  • Optional insurance gap coverage available
  • Broad carrier network

Cons

  • Pricing structure can be confusing without a discount
  • Customer service experience varies by region
6. Ship A Car Direct
★★★★☆ 4.4 / 5
Damage-free carrier vetting and documented condition reports

Ship A Car Direct built its reputation on one specific commitment: your car arriving in the same condition it left in. Carriers are vetted specifically for their damage-free track record, and photo documentation is standard at both pickup and delivery. For anyone shipping a vehicle they care about and wanting a clear paper trail, Ship A Car Direct is a strong, reliable option.

Pros

  • Damage-free carrier vetting is thorough
  • Photo documentation at both pickup and delivery
  • Strong customer review record
  • Responsive customer service

Cons

  • Not always the cheapest option
  • Pickup availability varies by route
7. Sherpa Auto Transport
★★★★☆ 4.3 / 5
Best for international shipments and price certainty

Sherpa stands out for international car shipping, guiding customers through the process via sister companies AES Shipping and AFL Vehicle Transport, with frequent status updates along the way. Roll-on/roll-off and container shipments are available, and even large trucks can be accommodated; air freight is offered as a pricier expedited option. Their Price Lock Promise adds up to $300 toward your quote to help ensure a carrier picks up your order on schedule, a useful hedge against the marketplace pricing volatility common in this industry.

Pros

  • International car shipping via sister companies
  • Price Lock Promise adds up to $300 toward your quote
  • Extended customer service hours, seven days a week
  • Roll-on/roll-off, container, and air freight options

Cons

  • Quotes tend to run higher than many competitors
  • No guaranteed pickup date
  • Does not service some specialized vehicle types
8. Passport Transport
★★★★☆ 4.1 / 5
Enclosed-only carrier with deep classic and exotic car experience

Passport Transport has specialized exclusively in enclosed transport since 1970, and that 55-plus year focus shows. As a direct carrier rather than a broker, they use their own fleet of custom-built, hard-sided, lift-gate trailers, with no upfront fees; payment is due only once your order is dispatched for pickup. Insurance coverage runs up to $1.5 million per vehicle, among the highest in the industry, and their driver training and equipment are built specifically around the needs of low-clearance, exotic, and classic vehicles. A standard seven-day pickup window applies, with date-specific pickup available for an additional fee.

Pros

  • Enclosed-only; over 55 years of specialization
  • Direct carrier with company-owned fleet
  • Up to $1.5 million in cargo insurance coverage
  • No upfront fees; pay once dispatched
  • Deep experience with classic, exotic, and collector vehicles

Cons

  • Enclosed only; no open carrier option
  • Standard 7-day pickup window; date-specific costs extra
  • Premium pricing relative to standard brokers
9. Auto Shipping Group
★★★☆☆ 3.9 / 5
Straightforward broker with both open and enclosed options

Auto Shipping Group has coordinated vehicle transport since 2003 and works with a wide network of vetted carriers across all 50 states, Puerto Rico, and select international destinations. Both open and enclosed transport are available, along with door-to-door service as standard. Every quote includes up to $100,000 in damage coverage, and a military discount is available. Pickup dates are not guaranteed, and a $129 cancellation fee applies once a carrier has been assigned.

Pros

  • Open and enclosed transport available
  • Up to $100,000 in damage coverage included
  • Door-to-door service as standard
  • Military discount available
  • Covers Puerto Rico and select international routes

Cons

  • No guaranteed pickup date
  • $129 cancellation fee after carrier is assigned
  • Some reports of price changes after initial quote
10. Mercury Auto Transport
★★★☆☆ 3.8 / 5
Broad vehicle type coverage including RVs and Guam shipping

Mercury handles a wide range of vehicle types beyond standard cars, including RVs, ATVs, and travel trailers, and ships to all 50 states plus Alaska, Hawaii, and Guam. A guaranteed delivery date option is available, which is uncommon in this industry. Vehicle tracking requires contacting the driver directly rather than an online portal, and standard insurance coverage is set at the FMCSA regulatory minimum unless supplemental coverage is arranged.

Pros

  • Handles non-standard vehicles including RVs and ATVs
  • Ships to Guam in addition to all 50 states
  • Guaranteed delivery date option available
  • Open, enclosed, and flatbed transport offered

Cons

  • No vehicle tracking portal; must contact driver directly
  • Insurance is FMCSA minimum only unless supplemented
  • No discounts advertised
