What to Know Before You Ship a Car

Auto transport pricing isn’t fixed — the same route can vary by hundreds of dollars depending on a handful of factors. Here’s what moves the price and how to pick the right carrier.

What Factors Affect the Price?

Distance & route

Long hauls cost more in total but less per mile. Busy lanes between major metros are cheapest; rural pickups or dropoffs add cost because fewer carriers run them.

Vehicle size & condition

Larger, heavier vehicles take more room on the trailer and raise the rate. Inoperable cars that need a winch cost extra to load.

Open vs. enclosed

Open transport is the cheapest and most common option. Enclosed typically runs 30–50% more but shields your vehicle from weather and road debris.

Time of year

Snowbird season and summer moving months push demand — and prices — up. Winter is generally cheaper outside southern routes.

Pickup speed

Standard pickup windows are the lowest cost. Guaranteed or expedited pickup dates carry a premium.

Door-to-door vs. terminal

Door-to-door delivery is the most convenient. Terminal-to-terminal can shave cost but means dropping off and collecting your car yourself.

Open vs. Enclosed Transport

Most vehicles ship just fine on an open carrier — it’s how new cars reach dealerships. Choose enclosed transport for classic, luxury, or high-value vehicles where protection from the elements is worth the added cost.

How to Choose a Car Shipping Company

✓Confirm the quote is all-in, with no deposit due until a carrier is assigned
✓Verify the company’s DOT and MC numbers and active insurance coverage
✓Read recent reviews on Google and the Better Business Bureau
✓Ask whether the quote is binding or an estimate that can change at pickup
✓Make sure door-to-door delivery is available on your specific route
