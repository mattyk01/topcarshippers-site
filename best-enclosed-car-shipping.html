Enclosed transport protects your vehicle from road debris, weather, and prying eyes. We evaluated seven companies on carrier quality, insurance coverage, and experience with high-value vehicles.

7 companies ranked · Updated April 2026 · No paid placements

Enclosed transport costs more than open carrier for a reason. Your vehicle travels in a covered trailer, shielded from weather, road debris, and the wear that open-air shipping exposes it to. For a daily driver, that may not matter much. For a luxury vehicle, a classic car, or anything you would not want exposed to highway conditions for several days, enclosed is the right call.

The seven companies on this list all handle enclosed transport. RoadRunner is our top pick; they have been shipping enclosed for 30 years and their vetted carrier network means the driver handling your vehicle has been screened, not just dispatched from an open marketplace.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about enclosed car shipping

What is enclosed car shipping?

Enclosed car shipping transports your vehicle in a covered trailer rather than on an open carrier. This protects it from weather, road debris, and visibility. It typically costs 30 to 50 percent more than open carrier.

When should I choose enclosed over open carrier?

Enclosed makes sense for luxury vehicles, classic or collector cars, exotic vehicles, or any car where condition on arrival is especially important. For most standard vehicles, open carrier is sufficient.

How much does enclosed car shipping cost?

Enclosed transport typically runs $200 to $500 more than open carrier for the same route. Cross-country enclosed shipping can range from $1,000 to $2,500 or more depending on vehicle type and timing.

Is enclosed shipping worth it for a regular car?

For most everyday vehicles, open carrier is sufficient and significantly cheaper. Enclosed is worth the premium when shipping something with higher value, sentimental significance, or condition sensitivity.
